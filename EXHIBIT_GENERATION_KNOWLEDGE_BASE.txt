## üóÇÔ∏è EXHIBIT STRUCTURE & ORGANIZATION

### Standard Exhibit Package Components (in order):

1. **Table of Contents** (first page)
   - Lists all exhibits with titles and descriptions
   - Shows exhibit letter/number
   - Includes page numbers for each exhibit

2. **Cover Letter / Support Letter** (if applicable)
   - Often Exhibit A
   - Petitioner's support letter
   - Consultation letters

3. **Forms** (early in package)
   - Form I-129 (Petition for Nonimmigrant Worker)
   - Form I-907 (Premium Processing, if applicable)
   - LCA (Labor Condition Application, if required)
   - Contract/Employment agreements

4. **Personal Documents** (typically early exhibits)
   - Passport (biographical pages and stamps)
   - Visa history documentation
   - Educational credentials (degrees, transcripts)
   - Professional licenses/certifications

5. **Brief / Legal Argument** (if included)
   - Detailed legal brief arguing the case
   - May be early (Exhibit A/B) or later depending on attorney preference
   - Typically 20-50 pages

6. **Evidence Organized by Criterion** (main body)
   - **O-1A**: 8 possible criteria (need 3)
     * (A) Nationally/Internationally Recognized Prizes or Awards
     * (B) Membership in Associations Requiring Outstanding Achievement
     * (C) Published Material About Beneficiary in Professional/Major Media
     * (D) Judging the Work of Others
     * (E) Original Contributions of Major Significance
     * (F) Authorship of Scholarly Articles
     * (G) Employment in Critical/Essential Capacity for Distinguished Organizations
     * (H) High Salary or Remuneration

   - **O-1B**: 6 possible criteria (need 3)
     * (A) Leading or Starring Role
     * (B) Critical Reviews or Published Materials
     * (C) Leading/Starring/Critical Role for Distinguished Organizations
     * (D) Record of Major Commercial or Critically Acclaimed Successes
     * (E) Significant Recognition from Organizations, Critics, Government, Experts
     * (F) High Salary/Remuneration Relative to Others

   - **EB-1A**: 10 possible criteria (need 3) OR one-time major award
     * (1) Receipt of Lesser Nationally/Internationally Recognized Prizes/Awards
     * (2) Membership in Associations Requiring Outstanding Achievement
     * (3) Published Material About the Beneficiary
     * (4) Participation as Judge of the Work of Others
     * (5) Original Contributions of Major Significance
     * (6) Authorship of Scholarly Articles
     * (7) Display at Artistic Exhibitions/Showcases
     * (8) Leading/Critical Role for Distinguished Organizations
     * (9) High Salary/Remuneration
     * (10) Commercial Success in Performing Arts

   - **P-1A**: Requires 2 of 7 criteria (athletes)
     * (1) Participation in Prior Season with Major U.S. Sports League/Team
     * (2) Participation with National Team in International Competition
     * (3) Participation in U.S. Intercollegiate Competition
     * (4) Written Statement from Governing Body Official
     * (5) Written Statement from Recognized Expert/Sports Media
     * (6) International Rankings
     * (7) Receipt of Significant Honors/Awards

7. **Supporting Documentation** (organized by criterion or category)
   - Media articles (organized chronologically or by significance)
   - Letters of recommendation/expert letters
   - Competition results
   - Awards and certificates
   - Contracts and financial documents
   - Publication documentation
   - Organization reputation evidence
   - Training facility credentials
   - Social media evidence
   - Statistical documentation

8. **Payment Evidence** (final exhibits, if applicable)
   - Filing fee receipt
   - Premium processing fee receipt
   - Credit card authorization

## üî§ EXHIBIT NUMBERING SYSTEMS

### System 1: Letter-Based (Most Common)
**Format**: Exhibit A, Exhibit B, Exhibit C, etc.
- **Use When**: Standard visa petition filings
- **Example Order**:
  - Exhibit A: Table of Contents
  - Exhibit B: Petitioner Support Letter
  - Exhibit C: Form I-129
  - Exhibit D: Beneficiary Passport
  - Exhibit E: Brief
  - Exhibit F: Awards (Criterion A)
  - Exhibit G: Media Coverage (Criterion C)
  - etc.

### System 2: Number-Based
**Format**: Exhibit 1, Exhibit 2, Exhibit 3, etc.
- **Use When**: Preference of specific attorney or simple sequential organization
- **Example Order**:
  - Exhibit 1: Table of Contents
  - Exhibit 2: Form I-129
  - Exhibit 3: Passport
  - etc.

### System 3: Criterion-Based Lettering
**Format**: Criterion A - Exhibit 1, Criterion A - Exhibit 2, etc.
- **Use When**: Multiple pieces of evidence for single criterion
- **Example**:
  - Criterion A (Awards): Exhibits A-1 through A-5
  - Criterion C (Media): Exhibits C-1 through C-12

### System 4: Roman Numeral
**Format**: Exhibit I, Exhibit II, Exhibit III, etc.
- **Use When**: Requested by specific attorney or formal preference

## üîç DOCUMENT-SPECIFIC ORGANIZATION RULES

### FORMS (always early in package)
**Order within Forms section**:
1. Main petition form (I-129 or I-140)
2. Premium processing form (I-907)
3. Supplement forms
4. Fee receipts

### PASSPORTS
**Organization**:
- Biographical page (first)
- All visa stamps (chronological)
- Entry/exit stamps (if relevant)
- Previous visa approvals (if extension)

### EDUCATIONAL CREDENTIALS
**Order**:
1. Highest degree first (PhD, then Master's, then Bachelor's)
2. Credential evaluations (if foreign degrees)
3. Transcripts (if relevant)
4. Professional licenses/certifications

### BRIEF/LEGAL ARGUMENT
**Standard Structure** (20-50 pages):
1. **Introduction** (1-2 pages)
   - Case overview
   - Visa category explanation
2. **Background** (2-4 pages)
   - Beneficiary biography
   - Professional history
3. **Legal Standards** (3-5 pages)
   - Regulatory framework
   - CFR citations
   - Applicable case law
4. **Statement of Facts** (3-5 pages)
   - Beneficiary accomplishments
   - Chronological narrative
5. **Criterion-by-Criterion Analysis** (15-30 pages)
   - Each criterion claimed
   - Evidence description
   - Regulatory compliance argument
6. **Final Merits Determination** (EB-1A only, 2-4 pages)
   - Totality of evidence
   - Sustained national/international acclaim
   - Standing at top of field
7. **Conclusion** (1 page)
   - Summary
   - Request for approval

### MEDIA ARTICLES
**Organization Rules**:
1. **By Significance** (Recommended):
   - Major international media first
   - National media second
   - Regional/specialized third

2. **By Date** (Alternative):
   - Chronological (newest first OR oldest first)

3. **By Topic** (Alternative):
   - Group by subject matter

**Article Format** (each article):
- Title page with source, date, author, URL
- Archived URL (archive.org)
- Complete article text
- Publication credibility page (circulation, reputation, reach)

### EXPERT LETTERS
**Organization**:
1. Expert credentials page (CV or bio)
2. Letter text
3. Expert-beneficiary relationship clarification
4. Expert's own recognition evidence

**Letter Requirements**:
- Letterhead (if organization-affiliated)
- Expert qualifications explicitly stated
- Specific examples of beneficiary's contributions
- Comparison to others in field
- Independent perspective (not employer/family)
- Original signature

### AWARDS DOCUMENTATION
**Per Award**:
1. Award certificate/trophy photo
2. Award description (from awarding organization)
3. Award significance evidence:
   - Selection process
   - Previous winners
   - Number of recipients
   - Geographic scope
4. Awarding organization credibility:
   - Organization background
   - Membership/reach
   - Industry standing
5. Media coverage of award (if available)

### ORGANIZATION REPUTATION
**Evidence Structure**:
1. Organization overview (About Us page)
2. Financial data (revenue, employees, market share)
3. Industry rankings/recognition
4. Client list / notable projects
5. Awards received by organization
6. Media coverage of organization

### TRAINING FACILITIES (Athletes)
**Evidence Structure**:
1. Facility overview
2. Notable athletes trained there
3. Championship records
4. Coach credentials and achievements
5. Facility rankings/reputation
6. Media coverage

## üí° BEST PRACTICES

### 1. **Exhibit Titles Should Be Descriptive**
**Good**: "Exhibit F: ESPN Feature Article - 'Rising Star in Mixed Martial Arts' (June 2024)"
**Bad**: "Exhibit F: Article"

### 2. **Organize Evidence by Strength**
Within each criterion, lead with your strongest evidence

### 3. **Include Context Pages**
Before each criterion section, include a brief context page explaining what follows

### 4. **Number All Pages**
Use consistent page numbering throughout (bottom center or bottom right)

### 5. **Use Divider/Separator Pages**
Between major sections or criteria, use separator pages for clarity

### 6. **Maintain Professional Formatting**
- Consistent fonts (Times New Roman 12pt or Arial 11pt)
- 1-inch margins
- Single or 1.15 line spacing
- Black text on white background

### 7. **Quality Over Quantity**
Focus on strong, relevant evidence rather than padding with weak materials

### 8. **Cross-Reference**
In the brief, reference specific exhibit letters/numbers when citing evidence

### 9. **Translations**
All non-English documents must include certified translations with translator credentials

### 10. **Final Package Check**
- Table of Contents matches actual exhibits
- Page numbers are accurate
- All exhibits referenced in brief are included
- No missing pages
- All required forms are signed
- Payment evidence is current

## üìû SUPPORT & TROUBLESHOOTING

### Common Questions:

**Q: What if I don't have evidence for all criteria?**
A: You only need 3 criteria for O-1A/O-1B, 3 for EB-1A, 2 for P-1A. Focus on your strongest.

**Q: Can I combine multiple pieces of evidence into one exhibit?**
A: Yes, especially for criterion-based organization. Example: "Exhibit F: Media Coverage (Articles 1-15)"

**Q: Should the brief be early or late in the package?**
A: Attorney preference varies. Common positions: Exhibit A/B (early) or after personal docs but before criterion evidence.

**Q: How many expert letters do I need?**
A: Quality over quantity. 3-5 strong independent expert letters better than 10 weak ones.

**Q: Can I use social media as evidence?**
A: Yes, if archived and part of demonstrating media coverage or public recognition. Must be credible sources.
